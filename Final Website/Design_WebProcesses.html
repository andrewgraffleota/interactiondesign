Steps to Web Design

Planning out a website


Ideation

In the name, you can see idea as part of ideation. That’s essentially what it is. Idea’s that are conversated over client interests for their website. Sparked by many questions along with many answers. “What message do we want, who are we attracting”


source: 12 steps to Virtual Ideation, CR Research

Personas

Creating different users with their own unique interests and seeing how a particular website will prove appeal based on several factors such as backgrounds, lifestyle, and goals etc.

Moodboard

A mood board usually has a nice splash of combinations to generate examples of how you slowly bring your elements to life.


Sitemap

You got your colours sorted, now it’s the structure of the website known as the hierarchy. You can see the branches and typically the amount of pages you’re creating with its own wonderful content.

source: Site Maps, Avasta

Wireframes

A step closer as you design your interface. It's literally is how you place your images, logos, headers, clicking stuff etc. A nice layout in the format of your website.


Prototyping

I like to call these the drafts. You have designed your first base website and now you can mix it up, refine it, until it meets your desired outcome.


Testing

source: Checklist for UI testing, Lvivity

The most important part of web design is testing, testing and testing. Making sure all buttons work, everything flows, sizing is appropriate, colours are even, no missing text or incorrect text and all links and navigation bar operate accordingly. A pedantic task that involves eye for precision and the ability to make changes when necessary.
